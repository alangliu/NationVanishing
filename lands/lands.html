Islands on the Brink

The islands of the Pacific are united by a common threat as some of the first countries in the world to face the most extreme effects of climate change.

The Pacific Atolls

Atolls are ring shaped coral reef islands connected by channels of open sea. The atolls in the Pacific Ocean are among the lowest land masses in the world, with the highest point on these islands often only a few meters above sea level.

The islands of Kiribati, the Marshall Islands and Tuvalu are particularly vulnerable as some of the first nations predicted to fully disappear off the map due to rising sea levels within the next century.

The King Tide

While complete disappearance of pacific islands may seem distant, the effects from climate change can be seen today. King tides are extremely high tides that are becoming more frequent in the Pacific region due to a warming climate. These tides break through seawalls, causing seawater to seep up through the center of the islands.
