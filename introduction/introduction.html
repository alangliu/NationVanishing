Introduction

Rising Waters

The story of rising sea levels begins with us. Human activity over the past 250 years has threatened our Earth's environment, lands, and people.

A Warming Planet

Our need for energy in a post-industrial world is greater than ever. Energy is used to power our cars, operate our factories, and bring electricity to our homes. Burning fossil fuels such as coal and oil releases carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gasses into our atmosphere, trapping heat on our Earth, and in turn, causing global sea levels to rise.

Scientists all around the world have agreed that recent climate change cannot be explained by natural causes alone and that human activity is the dominant cause for warming.

2 Degrees

Since 1990, a 2 degrees celsius benchmark has been set by world policymakers and scientists as the tipping point toward the most severe effects of climate change.

If global surface temperatures increase by even 2 degrees, the world's coral reefs would disappear and average sea level would rise more than 3 feet.
