The Last Resort

What happens when homelands become completely uninhabitable? The governing bodies of island nations must now take a stand to ensure their country's survival.

Climate Migration

As climate change threatens the long-term survival of the pacific islands, mass relocation of populations may soon become inevitable. Kiribati has become the first country to purchase land from a neighbouring nation, Fiji, to house future displaced citizens. Countries like Tuvalu and the Marshall Islands are seeking immigration agreements with Australia, New Zealand, and the United States.

A Plea for Action

COP21

Pacific leaders have turned to governments around the world to address the urgent issue of climate change. In 2015, Islanders united under a collective plea to over 190 countries gathered at the United Nations Climate Change Conference, or COP 21, in Paris to discuss landmark climate goals as a global community.
